Learn Telugu - Basics

1. Introduction to Telugu

Telugu is a Dravidian language spoken in the Indian states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. It has a rich literary tradition and is one of the official languages of India.

2. Common Phrases

Learning common phrases is a great way to start speaking Telugu.

Hello: నమస్తే (Namastē) (na-mas-tey)

Thank you: ధన్యవాదాలు (Dhanyavādālu) (dhan-ya-vaa-daa-lu)

Goodbye: వీడా (Vīḍā) (vee-da)

Yes: అవును (Avunu) (a-vu-nu)

No: కాదు (Kādu) (kaa-du)

3. Basic Vocabulary

Here are some basic words to build your Telugu vocabulary.

Water: నీరు (Nīru) (nee-roo)

Food: ఆహారం (Āhāram) (aa-haa-ram)

Friend: స్నేహితుడు (Snēhitudu) (sneh-ee-tu-doo)

Family: కుటుంబం (Kūṭumbam) (koo-tum-bam)

Car: కారు (Kāru) (kaa-roo)

4. Basic Grammar

Understanding basic grammar concepts will help you construct simple sentences in Telugu.

  • Subject-Verb-Object Order: Telugu follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) sentence structure, like English.
  • Genders: Telugu has three genders: masculine, feminine, and neuter.
  • Verb Conjugations: Verbs in Telugu conjugate based on tense, gender, and subject.

5. Pronunciation Tips

Pronunciation can be a challenge for new learners. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Listen to native speakers to understand the correct pronunciation.
  • Practice frequently to build familiarity with the sounds.
  • Use resources like online dictionaries or pronunciation guides.

Tip: Try speaking Telugu with a friend or language partner to improve your skills. Consistent practice is key!
